Important notes about booking your citizenship appointment

  • Please select the soonest available appointment that suits you.
  • Appointments run for approximately 2 hours.
  • You are required to schedule an appointment within 2 weeks of receiving this information.
  • You can reschedule your appointment up to 3 times and only 36hrs prior to your appointment. If you do not attend 2 appointments in total, a decision may be made on your application.

You must bring the following original documents or you may be unable to sit your citizenship test

Evidence of when you first arrived in Australia and your present country of citizenship

Current and previous passport, or
OR
Travel document with visa
E.g. Document for Travel to Australia (DFTTA), PLO56 (M56), Titre de Voyage

Birth certificate

Documents for children

The following documents are required for all children under 16 years of age

A full birth certificate or family register containing parent’s details
A Passport or travel document used to enter Australia, if not on your passport
Evidence of any name change between birth and current name, if applicable
If you have been granted custody and/or guardianship of the child, bring the relevant court orders.
If the child is adopted, bring the adoption papers.

If you have applied online

Form 1195 - Identity declaration
Bring the original form which you completed and attached to your online application.

If you have included children in your application they are not required to attend this appointment. There are no childcare facilities so you will need to make alternative childcare arrangements for your children.

Translating your documents

Documents in languages other than English must be accompanied by an English translation. If you are in Australia when applying, use translators accredited by the National Accreditation Authority of Translators and Interpreters (NAATI). For more information see www.naati.com.au.

If you are outside Australia when applying, the English translation must be completed by a qualified translator and must be endorsed by the translator with their full name, address, telephone number, and details of their qualifications and experience in the language being translated. Contact the Australian diplomatic mission in the country that issued the documents for advice on acceptable translators and who can certify your original documents.

How to prepare for the test

You will then have 45 minutes to complete the test which consists of 20 multiple choice questions. Australian Citizenship: Our Common Bond has all the information you need to help you pass the citizenship test.
